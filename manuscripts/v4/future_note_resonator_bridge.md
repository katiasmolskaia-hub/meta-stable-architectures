Katia suggested a useful practical analogy:
- an input resonator may help the group catch a clear common signal;
- an output or focal resonator may help hold attention and concentration;
- the two signals do not have to play the same role.

## Working interpretation

Possible separation:
- input resonator = clean timing cue for the group;
- output / focus resonator = stabilization or concentration cue.

The second cue may plausibly be:
- equal in frequency to the input cue if strict synchronization is needed;
- slightly lower / softer if the goal is retention of focus rather than hard entrainment.
