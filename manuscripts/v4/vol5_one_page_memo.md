# Volume V One-Page Memo

## What we have right now

Volume V is strongest when framed as:

`Adaptive RC as an Instructor Model for heterogeneous groups`

not as:

- a proof of strong long-horizon learning,
- a full group-memory theory,
- or a controller that already dominates all recovery metrics.

## Strongest current results

1. The fixed RC of Volume IV can be extended into a bounded state-aware coordinator.
2. Typed lessons (`noise`, `lag`, `contagion`) generate meaningfully different field regimes.
3. Agent-side attunement to the instructor signal reduces field error across repeated lessons.
4. This effect is visible even when recovery-time and tail metrics remain almost unchanged.

## What is weaker

1. `Instructor v2` does not yet clearly beat adaptive RC on recovery time or tail span.
2. `K_g` is plausible, but not yet the strongest practical result.
3. More complex response policies and multi-channel attunement did not yet justify their added complexity.

## Best current claim

`Mutual attunement between a bounded instructor-like coordinator and a heterogeneous group improves field quality under repeated lessons, even before large gains appear in coarse recovery-time metrics.`

## What this is useful for

This is not only a human metaphor.
It suggests a broader systems principle:

`distributed systems may first improve by reducing error and turbulence before they improve by becoming faster`

## Preprint status

Good candidate for a preprint if framed as:
- exploratory,
- conceptually coherent,
- simulation-based,
- and honest about current limits.

Not yet ideal if framed as:
- strong proof of instructor superiority,
- strong tail-learning result,
- or mature group-memory theory.

## Best next jump

If continuing after this plateau, the clean next target is:
- turbulence / cascade suppression,
or
- a stronger focus-lock mechanism under contagion.
